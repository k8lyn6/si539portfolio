Cooking

Being a grad student, I often find myself stressed out by my classes, internship searches, and part time job. One of my favorite stress relief activities is cooking! I learned how to make a few family recipes for the holidays as a child from my dad, but I did not begin to enjoy it until I started college and lived on my own. Now, I try to make a new recipe each week.

Meals

I follow a mostly vegetarian diet, and I like to cook with fresh and wholesome ingredients. One of my favorite recipe blogs is Cookie + Kate because Kate comes up with creative and delicious recipes to make with in-season produce (and her dog is adorable, too. I also am enjoying trying recipes from Budget Bytes. Budget Bytes fits a grad student's budget!

Baking

Raspberry thumbprint cookies from Sally's Baking Addiction

Cooking meals is fun, but baking is the ultimate stress relief. My favorite thing to bake is muffins, but I also enjoy decorating cupcakes and making gooey cookies to share with friends. Whenever I go to an event or party, I grab a recipe from Sally's Baking Addiction to bake because I know it will be a hit! If I'm preparing for a race and I want a healthier treat to make, I try something new from Chocolate Covered Katie. Katie's recipes are always inventive and delicious!

Bread

Baking bread is the ultimate stress relief with the highest reward. Although it is time consuming, I enjoy the process of kneading the dough, letting it rise, and watching it transform. My mom's friend taught me how to bake focaccia a few years ago, and I've been hooked ever since! When I moved to Michigan this year, I struggled with breadmaking, as my house is often too cold for the yeast to thrive!
