Hi! My name is Kaitlyn, and I am a first year master's student at the University of Michigan. I am studying Preservation of Information, and I am interested in the technical side of providing access to and preserving information. When I'm not in class or at my part-time job in the Special Collections Library, I enjoy cooking, running, and traveling.

Undergraduate Education

I completed my freshman year at Northwestern State University in Natchitoches, LA as part of the Scholar's College. I transferred to Rice University in Houston, TX my sophomore year and completed a Bachelor's degree in anthropology in 2014. While at Rice, I took classes in history, religion, English, and anthropology. I also participated in the Rice University Marching Owl Band and was a founding member of the Rice Quidditch League.

Work Experience

After graduating from Rice University, I worked as the program coordinator for Community Bridges, a service learning fellowship for undergraduates in the sociology department at Rice. I enjoyed working with our nonprofit community partners to ensure that the internship program met their needs and learning more about Houston as a city.

Graduate Education

After working for a year, I began as a master's student at the University of Michigan School of Information in 2015. I thought I would pursue a career path in a typical archive or special collections, but through taking more technical classes at SI, I became interested in careers within libraries and archives that require more technical knowledge.
